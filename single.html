Hadith (The Sayings and Actions of the Prophet Muhammad)

Admin

Web Design

15

Definition: Hadith refers to the sayings, actions, and approvals of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). It is the second most important source of guidance for Muslims after the Quran. Importance: Hadith helps explain and elaborate on the teachings found in the Quran. For example, it provides practical examples of how to follow Islamic principles in daily life—such as how to pray, fast, and perform charity.

Sahih: Authentic hadith. Da'if: Weak hadith. Mutawatir: A hadith that has been transmitted by a large number of people in every generation. Ahad: Hadith that has been transmitted by a few people. Famous Collections of Hadith: Sahih al-Bukhari Sahih Muslim Sunan Abu Dawood Jami` at-Tirmidhi .

Fiqh

Definition: Fiqh is the understanding and interpretation of Islamic law. It covers all aspects of a Muslim's life, including acts of worship (such as prayer, fasting, and charity) and social interactions (such as marriage, business, and contracts). Importance: Fiqh provides practical guidelines for Muslims to live in accordance with Islamic teachings. It allows scholars to derive legal rulings from the Quran and Hadith to deal with new situations in society. Main Schools of Fiqh: Hanafi: One of the four major schools of Sunni jurisprudence. It is the most widespread, especially in South Asia and Turkey. Maliki: Popular in North and West Africa. Shafi'i: Common in Southeast Asia, East Africa, and parts of Egypt. Hanbali: Found mainly in Saudi Arabia and parts of the Arabian Peninsula

Tauhid

Definition: Tawhid is the belief in the absolute oneness of Allah. It is the core concept of Islam and signifies that Allah has no partners, no equals, and no competitors. Importance: Tawhid is the foundation of the Islamic faith. It is the belief that there is only one God (Allah), who is the creator, sustainer, and ruler of the universe. Belief in Tawhid is the essence of the Shahada (the Islamic declaration of faith). Types of Tawhid: Tawhid ar-Rububiyyah: The belief in the Lordship of Allah (i.e., that Allah is the creator and sustainer of everything). Tawhid al-Uluhiyyah: The belief that only Allah is worthy of worship. Tawhid al-Asma wa-Sifat: The belief that Allah’s names and attributes are unique and match His perfection. .
